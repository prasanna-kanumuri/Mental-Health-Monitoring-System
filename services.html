The MINDS Foundation

Founded in 2010, The MINDs Foundation is headquartered in Nizamabad, and has locations in Mumbai, Vadodara and Bhavnagar. Since its inception, the organisation has been working tirelessly to increase awareness around mental illness and provide more access to healthcare. Committed to eliminating the stigma that surrounds mental illness in India, it follows a three-phase approach. The first phase shines the spotlight on the importance of education. The MINDS Foundation has started a program to educate communities in villages across India. With the help of social workers, MINDs staff and Community Mental Health Workers, they raise awareness about the basics of mental illness and the effectiveness of treatment. In the second phase, they focus on effective, free treatment for any mentally ill person, and in last phase, the team works with each patient post-treatment to reintegrate them into their local communities.

Email: info@mindsfoundation.org

Address: The MINDS Foundation India, 1-51/46/2C, Bank Colony, Mahalaxmi Nagar, Pangra, Nizamabad, Telangana

Anjali Mental Health Rights Organisation

Based in Kolkata, the Anjali Mental Health Rights Organisation works to humanise mental health and create awareness about mental disabilities at the grassroots level in partnership with local, state and national governments. Founded by Ratnaboli Ray, the organisation aspires to make people with psycho-social disability aware of their rights, and also secure large-scale policy shifts in the country that establish mental health as a priority for development. The organisation is up and running in three hospitals in West Bengal—Pavlov Mental Hospital, Lumbini Park Mental Hospital and Bahrampur Mental Hospital—and works with patients to create a comprehensive package of healthcare services and therapies to supplement those already available in government hospitals.

Email: info@anjalimentalhealth.org

Address: 93/2, Kankulia Road, Benubon, A302, Kolkata, West Bengal

Sangath Mental Health Rights Organisatio

A non-governmental, not-for-profit organisation, Sangath works to promote good health—physical, psychological and social—of children, adolescents and adults. Their primary areas of focus include child development, adolescent and youth health and adult mental health.

The work of the foundation is driven by four key principles. They want to mix-up the social, psychological and medical interventions that are needed to improve community health, and use a diverse range of academic and health disciplines to generate effective treatment. They insist on working with the best evidence available to them and thoroughly evaluate and disseminate every case they receive.

With an aim to develop a model of care that is scaled up through government machinery, they collaborate with government health services, department of education, schools, other NGOs and the Goa Medical College to provide the very best for their patients.

The Banyan

A Chennai-based organisation, The Banyan was set up in 1993 by Vandana Gopikumar and Vaishnavi Jayakumar, with the goal of approaching mental health through the well-being and development paradigm, and not within the framework of an illness. The organisation endeavours to cater to the mentally ill and homeless women in the city as they create replicable and sustainable models of care that address the interconnected elements of mental health.

The foundation provides emergency care and critical time interventions in two hospitals and has reached over 3,400 individuals so far. Working under the belt of the National Urban Livelihoods Mission (NULM), they provide free-of-charge mental health and social care services, address distress, and decode both common mental disorders and severe mental disorders among women living in abject poverty.

The organisation also works with women who require long-term care and offers long-term living options and supportive care to help them get back on their feet.

Schizophrenia Research Foundation (SCARF)

Established in 1984, SCARF was founded by Dr. Sarada Menon, the first woman psychiatrist in the country, to provide rehabilitation and care to individuals suffering from severe mental disorders. The organisation has worked to provide an out-patient department, a daycare centre, and an in-patient facility at the Mental Health Center in Anna Nagar, and two residential rehabilitation centres at Thiruverkadu and Mahabalipuram for individuals who are suffering from mental health troubles.

The organisation has provided free treatment to over 25,000 people till date, as well as free-of-charge rehabilitation. They have a special clinic for Early Intervention in Psychosis and also provide education, training, and care for patients suffering from dementia. Plus, the organisation trains different health professionals in mental health too, to sensitize them and help them treat patients better.
