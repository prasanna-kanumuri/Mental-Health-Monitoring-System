Yoga Mudras





1.GYAN / JNANA MUDRA [Knowledge Mudra]

This is one of the most fundamental yoga mudras for increasing concentration and knowledge. Sit comfortably in a meditation pose such as Padmasana or Sukhasana. Your index fingers should be folded such that they touch the inside root of your thumbs. Straighten each hand's remaining three fingers so that they are relaxed and slightly apart. Now, with the palms facing down, place the hands on the knees. Hands and arms should be relaxed.

2.CHIN MUDRA [CONSCIOUSNESS Mudra]

Gently touch the tip of your thumb to the tip of your index finger, and let the other three fingers remain straight but relaxed. This mudra is believed to improve mental clarity and increase alertness.This mudra is often practiced during meditation to enhance concentration, promote a sense of calm and connection with the divine, and channel energy inward. It is associated with wisdom and knowledge, making it a common choice for those seeking to deepen their meditation practice and tap into their inner wisdom.

3.APANA MUDRA [ENERGY Mudra]

Join the tips of your thumb, middle finger, and ring finger while keeping the index and pinky fingers extended. Apana mudra is thought to help with mental and emotional balance, reducing stress, and promoting a sense of grounding.

4.DHYANA MUDRA [CONSCIOUSNESS Mudra]

Dhyana Mudra is a hand gesture commonly used in meditation practices. It involves placing both hands on the lap, right hand resting on top of the left, with palms facing upwards and thumbs lightly touching each other to form a triangular shape. This mudra symbolizes concentration, inner peace, and a state of deep meditation, making it an essential element in various spiritual and mindfulness traditions.

5.SHUNYA MUDRA [Heavenly Void Mudra]

Bend your middle finger and place the pad of your thumb against the middle finger's second joint. This mudra is believed to alleviate emotional imbalances and reduce feelings of emptiness or hollowness.

6.PRANA MUDRA [Vitality Mudra]

Join the tips of your ring finger and little finger to the tip of your thumb while keeping the other fingers extended. Prana mudra is thought to boost vitality, reduce fatigue, and improve overall mental and physical energy.

7.VAYU MUDRA

Vayu Mudra, a hand gesture in yoga, is performed by joining the tip of the index finger to the base of the thumb while keeping the other fingers extended. It is believed to help alleviate excessive air-related issues in the body, such as gas and bloating, by balancing the Vayu or air element. Practicing Vayu Mudra is thought to promote better digestion and reduce anxiety.
